## Summary

Together, the 2023 life-balance deck, the LEAF 2026 deck, and the FMIPA industrial project workbook form a layered account of the "FMIPA Experiment." The 2023 deck supplies an earlier Tridharma and lecturer-balance framing, the LEAF deck supplies the management thesis and experimental framing, and the workbook supplies a source dataset for the industrial/applied project portfolio while introducing numeric reconciliation questions.

## Synthesis

- The 2023 life-balance deck shows an early narrative where industrial collaboration is part of a balanced lecturer identity across teaching, research, community service, global participation, and leadership.
- The deck's management argument is that a shift in primary KPI can change institutional behavior without sacrificing scientific output.
- The workbook supports the existence of a substantial applied portfolio: 82 rows, 2022-2026 project years, and a large funding column total.
- The strongest shared theme across both sources is applied geophysics and energy-sector collaboration, especially passive seismic, seismic processing, reservoir work, geophysical software, and related field systems.
- The deck broadens the story from a project portfolio into a leadership hypothesis: industry funding, product adoption, and community impact can become signals that organize academic behavior.
- The current evidence is strong enough for an internal knowledge base and draft narrative, but not yet clean enough for final public numbers without reconciliation.

## Key Reconciliation Points

- Contract value: deck says Rp 184 billion; workbook extraction totals `180,575,375,943` in the Funding column.
- Currency bridge: the 2023 life-balance deck reports 2022 and 2023 industrial contract totals in USD, while the workbook funding column has no stated currency.
- Year counts: deck sequence is 9, 12, 30, 26, then growing; workbook extraction is 9, 12, 18, 38, and 5.
- Geophysics 2025: deck slide says Rp 75 billion; speaker notes mention 89 billion.
- Publication control: deck cites Scival FMIPA, but no underlying Scival capture has been ingested.
