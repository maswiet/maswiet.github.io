## Summary

This 18-slide deck for LEAF 2026 frames FMIPA UGM's 2022-2026 research-management shift as an "experiment" in which the Vice Dean's primary KPI is redefined from publication output toward industry funding, product adoption, and community impact, while publication output remains the control variable that must not degrade.

The deck identifies [[wiki/entities/wiwit-suryanto]] as the speaker and Vice Dean for Research and Community Services at [[wiki/entities/fmipa-ugm]]. It presents the experiment as a faculty-level case, reports industrial collaboration outcomes, lists material outputs, and invites collaboration on a larger national-scale experiment.

## Key Points

- Event context: LEAF 2026 at Universitas Indonesia, dated 2026-07-08 to 2026-07-09 in the deck.
- Presentation premise: scientists can run experiments not only in laboratories but also on the systems that govern laboratories.
- KPI shift: conventional publication, patent, h-index, citation, and ranking metrics are contrasted with experimental KPIs for industry funding value, product adoption, and community impact.
- Hypothesis: if the primary research-management KPI shifts toward industry funding, product adoption, and community impact, researcher behavior, collaboration structures, and resource flows should shift without degrading scientific quality.
- Setup: one faculty, FMIPA UGM; period 2022-2026; intervention is redefining the Vice Dean's primary KPI; publication output is treated as the control.
- Reported outcomes: 82 industrial collaboration projects, Rp 184 billion total contract value, about USD 11.2 million, and 30+ projects per year in 2024-2025.
- Reported single-field validation: geophysics alone generated Rp 75 billion in 2025.
- Material outputs named: SpectraGAMA v4.0, PertaGAMANT v3.0, Tarsius, Geophysical UAV, Ocean Bottom Seismometer, and MinerbaOne.
- Critical-control claim: publication output did not decline, and researchers most active in industry partnerships publish more; the deck cites "Scival FMIPA" but no separate Scival source was provided.
- Sub-experiment: the UGM-MFRI-JICA volcanic-disaster mitigation partnership is presented as evidence that long partnerships compound from 2014 onward.
