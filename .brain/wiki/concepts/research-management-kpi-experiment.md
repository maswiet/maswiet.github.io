## Summary

A research-management KPI experiment is a deliberate change in what a research organization primarily measures, followed by observation of how researcher behavior, collaboration structures, resource flows, product adoption, and scientific quality respond. In the LEAF 2026 deck, FMIPA UGM is presented as a faculty-level experimental site where the Vice Dean's primary KPI shifted toward industry funding, product adoption, and community impact while publication output remained a critical control.

## Key Points

- The deck's core causal proposition is that "what we measure determines what we grow."
- The experiment does not reject conventional scholarly metrics; it reframes them as necessary but insufficient for the management question being tested.
- The proposed dependent outcomes include industry collaboration value, project count, product deployments, community impact, and publication output as a control.
- The associated workbook gives source data for an industry-facing applied project portfolio, but its totals and year counts do not fully align with the deck's reported figures.
