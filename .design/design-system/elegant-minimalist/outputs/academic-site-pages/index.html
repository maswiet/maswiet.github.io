Geophysics · Universitas Gadjah Mada

Reading the earth through its tremors.

I study seismology and volcano geophysics — how seismic waves reveal the structure beneath Java, and how continuous monitoring can sharpen our understanding of active volcanoes and earthquake hazard.

My work sits between fieldwork and computation: deploying seismic arrays, building open processing tools, and teaching the next generation of geophysicists at UGM.

Selected research

All projects →

  • Merapi volcano seismic monitoring

    • Volcano seismology
    • Monitoring

    Continuous broadband recording and event classification to track shallow activity and improve short-term hazard assessment at one of the world's most active volcanoes.

  • Ambient noise tomography of Java

    • Tomography
    • Crustal structure

    Using cross-correlation of ambient seismic noise to image crustal velocity structure and sedimentary basins across central Java.

  • Low-cost seismic instrumentation

    • Instrumentation
    • Open hardware

    Developing affordable sensor and data-logging setups so more schools and regional stations can contribute to seismic networks.
